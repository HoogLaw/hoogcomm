Accessibility Practice

Accessibility is rapidly becoming one of the most important competitive drivers in consumer technology. HoogComm's Accessibility Practice — newly launched — helps organizations move beyond compliance and feature lists toward meaningful, community-informed engagement. Our approach combines 25 years of experience advising global consumer electronics brands and industry coalitions with lived-experience leadership from Tyler Hoog, a disability advocate and consultant who has worked with Disney, Netflix, Marvel, NBC, and Apple.

Accessibility is not a campaign. It is a relationship. Relationships require trust, consistency, and the involvement of people who live the experience.

Communications Strategy & Narrative

We position accessibility as a genuine business commitment, not a regulatory footnote. Narrative development, executive messaging, media strategy, and content that gives a client's accessibility work a credible public voice — including internal communications, because a message that lives only in marketing rarely survives contact with the rest of the organization.

Community Engagement & Advocacy Liaison

Credibility in the disability space is earned through relationships, not announcements. We connect clients with the advocates and organizations whose trust reflects the specific audience — including the Christopher and Dana Reeve Foundation, DisabilityBelongs, and Swim with Mike — and structure those partnerships as genuine feedback loops, not photo opportunities.

Influencer & Creator Strategy

Disabled creators have built some of the most trusted audiences in the accessibility space. We identify the right voices, structure authentic partnerships, and develop content programs across YouTube, TikTok, and social that reach mobility, hearing, vision, and cognitive communities. We also support visibility at events and in retail where accessibility is increasingly a purchase driver.

Engagement Advisory Structure

For clients who need community input built into their process, we design and staff purpose-built advisory structures around a specific product, campaign, or initiative. Voices are selected for relevance, not availability. Members participate in product testing, reviews, and public programs — turning community input from a box to check into a source of real market intelligence.
